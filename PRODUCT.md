# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Clavix serves self-directed US investors, typically ages 45-65, managing roughly $500K-$5M+ in personal assets. They are financially literate, time-sensitive, and skeptical of retail-fintech hype. They usually check markets in the early morning on a phone, want fast clarity on what changed overnight, and expect drill-down depth when something looks materially worse.

## Product Purpose
Clavix exists to compress the investor's morning risk-review workflow into a credible mobile product. It tells the user what changed across macro conditions, sectors, and held names; assigns a transparent risk grade to tracked securities; and makes the evidence behind each rating inspectable. Success means a user can open the app, understand whether their portfolio materially changed, and trust the answer without feeling manipulated, rushed, or sold to.

## Brand Personality
Calm, precise, institutional. Clavix should feel like a compact rating-agency terminal, not a trading app, chatbot, or research newsletter. The emotional outcome is confidence through evidence: serious enough for skeptical investors, clear enough for mobile use, and restrained enough to feel premium.

## Anti-references
- Retail-trading energy such as Robinhood-style gamification, dopamine colors, or celebratory motion
- Generic AI-finance UI patterns such as purple gradients, glowing cards, chat-assistant framing, or faux-intelligent copy
- Wellness-style reassurance, soft lifestyle metaphors, or emotionally anthropomorphic portfolio language
- Black-box risk products that hide methodology, fake prior scores, or blur limited-data states

## Design Principles
- Lead with what changed: every primary screen should help the user answer "what matters now?" before it asks for deeper exploration.
- Evidence before interpretation: ratings, alerts, and article summaries should always imply inspectable underlying data.
- Trust through restraint: the UI should feel composed, sharp, and institutional, with motion and color reserved for meaning.
- Never fake certainty: missing history, thin coverage, and degraded-mode data must be explicit and visually honest.
- Optimize for the morning check-in: the first 30 seconds on mobile should be useful even for a busy or distracted user.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Clavix should meet a practical WCAG AA bar for contrast, text legibility, and touch-target sizing. Reduced motion support is required. Financial language should stay precise but not needlessly jargony, especially in onboarding, alerts, and limited-data states. Critical information must not rely on color alone, and grade/risk changes should remain understandable for color-blind users and older users on smaller screens.
